01 — The problem

You don't have a demand problem. You have a ceiling.

You built something that works — real clients, real reputation, $10–20k months. But every time you push for the next level, you hit the same wall. More clients just means more of you — more QA, more decisions routing to your desk, the whole thing held together with duct tape. You're the human router, and you wrote a 100+ page SOP and still can't let go — because a doc can't transfer your judgment.

Demand isn't the issue. Capacity is. Revenue goes up and the complexity eats the margin. You can feel it becoming the ceiling — the reason you can't get to the next milestone. That's not a discipline problem. It's structural.

02 — The reframe

The ceiling isn't your market. It's your head.

The next milestone doesn't come from more leads or more hustle — you've already proven you can sell. It comes from a business that can hold more than you can remember. Right now every standard and judgment call lives in your head, so you can only grow as fast as you can personally touch.

That's the ceiling — and it's a context problem, not an intelligence or discipline one. Your business is simply missing a second brain.

A second brain is how you break the ceiling.
